Call for proposals

Submission Formats

Short papers (2 pages + references) including demos and ​work-in-progress.


Full papers (up to 6 pages + references) including novel ​research papers, reviews of existing tools and methods, ​benchmark open datasets to catalyze research, evaluation of ​impact of AI supported tools for teaching and learning on ​outcomes.


The top 10 submissions will be exhibited as posters.

Submissions in PMLR Style

All submissions must follow the PMLR style template. ​Accepted full papers will be invited to submit an extended ​version, addressing the remarks of the reviewers, to PMLR to ​be published as part of the Workshop Proceedings.

Double-blind Submissions on OpenReview

All submissions will be evaluated through a double-blind ​review. All submissions must be made through the ​OpenReview portal for the workshop. Authors must have an ​OpenReview account in order to make submissions. Please ​adhere to the submission guidelines outlined on the workshop ​website.

TOPICS OF INTEREST

Cognitive Science Foundation: Applying learning science to ​enhance GenAI-supported teaching and learning frameworks.


Improving Language Models: Developing algorithms and fine-​tuning for better educational tasks.


Responsible Innovation: Embedding privacy, fairness, and ​transparency into AI-driven education.


Content Development: Creating and assessing educational content ​through AI.


Personalization: Leveraging AI for adaptive curricula and ​personalized learning.


Impact Evaluation: Measuring AI's effect on learning outcomes and ​career growth.


Educator Support: AI tools for evaluating student work and ​instructional practices.


Academic Integrity: Ensuring fairness and preventing misuse of AI-​generated content.


Benchmark Datasets: Creating fair, secure, and representative ​datasets for educational AI applications.
